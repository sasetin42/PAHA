1. Bones, Breaks, and Breakthroughs: Inside PAHA's Orthopedic Trauma Seminar
Here's what went down inside one of veterinary medicine's most intensive learning events of the year that happened from March 26-30, 2026.

When a small animal suffers orthopedic trauma, every decision counts — from the first assessment to the final stitch. That's exactly why the Philippine Animal Hospital Association (PAHA) brought together 77 veterinary professionals for a five-day deep dive into Managing Orthopedic Trauma in Small Animals: Improving Outcomes and Minimizing Complications.
Held at the PAHA Center and led by the highly respected Dr. Mark Glyde, this seminar isn't just a lecture series — it's a full immersive, case-based learning experience designed to sharpen clinical thinking and elevate the standard of small animal orthopedic care across the Philippines.

Day 1 & 2: Setting the Foundation
The seminar opened strong, drawing PCCAP Diplomates, NOVSI Batch 2, and veterinary professionals ready to go beyond the basics. Dr. Glyde guided participants through the core principles of orthopedic trauma — covering surgical planning, fracture repair, fixation techniques, and the nuanced management of complications, infections, and post-operative care.
But learning wasn't the only milestone. Days 1 and 2 also marked the signing of the PAHA x NOVSI Memorandum of Understanding — a significant step in strengthening collaboration between two key pillars of veterinary education in the country. A landmark moment, signed and sealed in the spirit of shared growth.

Day 3: Deeper, Sharper, Better
Momentum only grew on Day 3. Participants moved from foundational principles into more complex territory — refining surgical decision-making, troubleshooting difficult fracture cases, and developing sharper instincts for post-operative outcomes.
This is where theory starts becoming instinct. Real cases. Real decisions. Real learning.

Day 4 & 5: Mastery in Motion
By Day 4, the room felt different — the kind of quiet confidence that only comes from days of intensive, focused learning. Participants tackled advanced case integration, pushing their clinical approach further in fracture management, complication control, and post-operative strategy.
The highlight? A heartfelt awarding of Certificates of Appreciation — recognizing the dedication and active participation of every professional who showed up, leaned in, and committed to growing their craft.
It was more than a certificate. It was recognition of a community that refuses to settle for anything less than excellent care.
Five days. More than fifty professionals. Countless lives — both human and animal — changed by the knowledge gained inside that room.
This is what PAHA stands for. Not just seminars and certificates, but a relentless commitment to pushing veterinary medicine forward — one fracture, one case, one breakthrough at a time.

2. One Shot at a Time– PAHA Joins the Fight for a Rabies-Free Philippines
A nationwide movement. A united mission. 

Rabies is preventable. Yet every year, it continues to claim lives — and behind every case is a gap in protection that should never have existed. That's why on the day of the Nationwide Simultaneous Rabies Vaccination Drive– March 2026, members of the Philippine Animal Hospital Association (PAHA) showed up — not just as veterinarians, but as advocates for a safer, healthier Philippines.

One Campaign. One Vision.
Rallying behind the Philippine Veterinary Medical Association's powerful campaign cry — "A Rabies-Free Future Starts with Me" — PAHA members across the country rolled up their sleeves and got to work. Clinics opened their doors. Vets hit the ground. And one by one, animals were protected.
Because that's what it takes. Not one grand gesture, but thousands of small, deliberate acts of care — repeated, consistently, until the threat is gone.

Bigger Than a Vaccination Drive
This wasn't just a single-day event. It was a declaration.
In alignment with the World Organization for Animal Health's global goal to eliminate dog-mediated rabies by 2030, PAHA and its members are doing their part — bridging the gap between global ambition and local action. Every vaccine administered brings the Philippines one step closer to a milestone that will save both animal and human lives for generations to come.
No mission this big is accomplished alone. It takes veterinarians, communities, government agencies, and animal welfare advocates all moving in the same direction — and that's exactly what this drive represented.
PAHA's participation is a testament to what's possible when veterinary professionals unite around a common purpose: protecting communities, one vaccination at a time.

A Rabies-Free Future Starts Here
The goal is clear. The deadline is 2030. And the work — the real, meaningful, life-saving work — is already well underway.
To every PAHA member who participated, to every pet owner who brought their animal in, and to every partner who made this drive possible — thank you. You are proof that a rabies-free Philippines isn't just a dream.
It's a mission in motion.

3. Global Standards, Local Impact: WSAVA Continuing Education Comes to Manila
When the world's best veterinary minds meet Filipino passion for animal care — remarkable things happen.

On the evening of March 13, the Philippine Animal Hospital Association (PAHA), in partnership with AVLI Biocare, Inc., brought one of veterinary medicine's most prestigious learning experiences to the heart of Manila — the World Small Animal Veterinary Association (WSAVA) Continuing Education Event.
Held at the elegant Diamond Hotel Manila, what unfolded was more than a seminar. It was an evening of global insight, meaningful connection, and a shared commitment to raising the bar of animal care in the Philippines.

A Night Where Global Meets Local
The room was filled with passionate veterinary leaders, practitioners, and advocates — all united by one goal: to keep learning, keep growing, and keep elevating the standard of care for small animals across the country.
The highlight of the evening was a powerful talk from none other than Dr. Jim Berry, President of WSAVA — bringing a truly global perspective to a deeply local mission. His insights set the tone for an evening rich with expert discussions, practical clinical knowledge, and inspired conversation about the future of veterinary medicine in the Philippines.
What made this event especially significant was the strength of collaboration behind it. Beyond PAHA and AVLI Biocare, Inc., the evening was made possible in partnership with two key pillars of the Philippine veterinary community:
Philippine Veterinary Dental College (PVDCO)
Philippine College of Companion Animal Practitioners (PCCAP)
Together, these organizations brought their networks, their expertise, and their shared vision under one roof — proof that when the veterinary community unites, the impact is far greater than the sum of its parts.

From Global Insights to Clinical Practice
The evening wasn't just inspiring — it was practical. Speakers delivered talks that bridged the gap between international veterinary standards and everyday clinical realities in the Philippines. From cutting-edge global insights to actionable approaches practitioners could bring back to their clinics, every discussion added real value to the professionals in the room.
This is continuing education done right — not just information, but transformation.
Events like this are a reflection of PAHA's unwavering commitment: to connect Filipino veterinary professionals to the best knowledge, the best networks, and the best standards the world has to offer.
Because when vets grow, animals thrive. And when animals thrive, communities flourish.

4. Salu-Salo: Where Friendship Became a Global Veterinary Movement
One night. One historic signing. One vision. 

There's something uniquely Filipino about the word salu-salo — it speaks of gathering, of warmth, of coming together over a shared table. And on the evening of March 11, 2026, that spirit took on a meaning far greater than anyone could have scripted.
At the iconic Manila Hotel, the Philippine Animal Hospital Association (PAHA) hosted Salu-Salo: A Night of Global Veterinary Friendship — an evening where the warmth of Filipino hospitality met the weight of international purpose.

A Table Set for Leaders
The room brought together some of the most respected names in veterinary medicine. PAHA President Dr. Luchi Orlanda welcomed guests alongside the leaders of the World Small Animal Veterinary Association (WSAVA), headlined by WSAVA President Dr. Jim Berry — a gathering that reflected just how far the Philippine veterinary community has come on the global stage.
But this wasn't just a night of pleasantries and speeches. It was a night of friendship forged with intention — where shared values around animal care, professional excellence, and cross-border collaboration filled every conversation at every table.

The Moment That Made History
The defining highlight of the evening was an agreement signing ceremony that will be remembered as a landmark moment for Philippine veterinary medicine.
PAHA President Dr. Luchi Orlanda and Korean Animal Hospital Association (KAHA) President Dr. Yidon Choi signed a formal partnership agreement — uniting two nations under a shared commitment to advancing veterinary care across borders.
More than ink on paper, it was a promise of knowledge exchange, collaborative growth, and a future where Filipino and Korean veterinary professionals learn from and elevate one another.
As the evening drew to a close, what lingered wasn't just the memory of a beautiful dinner at one of Manila's most storied venues. It was the feeling of something larger taking shape — a veterinary community stepping boldly into its global identity, one meaningful partnership at a time.
Friendship brought us to the table. Purpose will carry us forward.
